What changed and why each refinement matters
1. Analytics line integrated with the L's foot. The line now emerges from the base of the L rather than floating independently in the lower-right. This makes the mark feel like a single designed object rather than a monogram with a separate decoration sitting next to it. It also gives the brand name a literal visual meaning — the Line in Independent Baseball Projections becomes the analytics line.

2. Wordmark split: FAIR light / LINE bold. "FAIR" in weight 300 (muted) and "LINE" in weight 900 (full white) creates a reading hierarchy that draws the eye through the name. It also reinforces the product concept — fair line — without having to explain it. This is more memorable than a single-weight block.

3. Deeper blue on the L (#60a5fa). The previous #93c5fd was so pale it looked like the L was slightly different white rather than a deliberate design choice. The new value has clear presence — you immediately read it as a two-tone monogram. It also aligns with the electric blue in the brand color system.
